Escape Room Marshfield Puzzles

I was intially contracted for this project by Escape Room Marshfield in the Summer of 2023. The project consisted of making a number of digital puzzles to be used in their next escape room. One of the unique challenges of the project was developing a physical interface for players to interact with while solving the puzzles. I made use of the makey-makey board to map buttons to computer inputs like the arrow keys and mouse buttons. Using this strategy I can place a button on each wall of the room, forcing one player to be on each button, the players are forced to communicate with each other to solve the puzzles. This adds a new fun and interesting level of complexity to seemingly easy puzzles. Designing this game helped me refine not only programming skills, but helped to reinforce a design philosophy of focusing on designing for my audience and making mechanics designed towards that audinece rather than a cool, complex mechanics that may not be applicable a given audience.

The project was displayed at the MAGD expo in 2024 and recieved a honorable mention for best in show for Real World Application. The full room is expected to open in June of 2025.

"Mobile" Escape Room

Separately from Escape Room Marshfield, I am developing a small scale collapsable escape room to show these puzzles as well as some other physical puzzles. The small room will be 12' x 12' and 8' tall. Each wall of the room will consist of three 4' wide and 8' tall segments that are connected via hinges on the outside as well as wooden dowels that fit between the walls. This is currently being developed to be shown at the 2025 Midwest Gaming Classic show April 4-6.
