UWW Summer Game Development Camp 2023

For two weeks in the summer of 2023 I worked with Professor Nick Hwang and another student, Brian Michael, to help guide both middle and high school students through the typical game design process. I worked as the art lead for the course helping students work with Photoshop and Piskel to create their own art and animations for their games. I worked to help instill principles of art and animation techniques while also trying to get the kids to be able to communicate effectively when talking about the technical aspects of art and game design. I also spent a decent portion of the time helping with the custom scripting that was needed for numerous games. I worked on automatic targeting for tower defense games, multiple controller input controls, and several others features that would help provide a basis for the students to utilize.

The camp was a great experience for me to develop and hone the necessary skills for the game industry. While teaching at the camp I was able to understand the vitality of communication in the game industry, as being able to communicate in effective terms was important to keeping everything clear to all people involved in the projects. I was also able to experience a much wider array of problems then I had been ale to before. With so many different games being develped there was so much new things that I had to find new and ineresting solutions to.
